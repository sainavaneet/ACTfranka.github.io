Abstract

In this project, we adapt the Action Chunk Transformer (ACT), originally designed for mobile robotic platforms as demonstrated in the Mobile Aloha framework, for use in industrial robotics, specifically targeting the Franka robot for pick-and-place tasks. The ACT model is renowned for its ability to enhance sequence learning and interactive capabilities in dynamic environments, attributes crucial for the precision and adaptability required in industrial settings.

To achieve this adaptation, we modified the ACT’s architecture to meet the unique kinematic and sensory requirements of the Franka robot. This allowed the robot to efficiently process and execute complex sequences of spatial tasks. Our approach involved integrating aspects of transformer technology, which are pivotal for managing sequential and contextual data in real time.

Initial results indicate a marked improvement in the robot’s task execution speed and accuracy, showcasing the transformative potential of transformer technology in robotic systems. This adaptation not only extends the functional reach of the ACT model from mobile to stationary robots but also highlights the broader applicability and significant benefits of transformer-based models in advancing robotic autonomy and operational efficiency in complex industrial environments.
